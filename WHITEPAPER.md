## Abstract
This paper formalizes the **Mercy Directive**, an alignment framework designed for autonomous, self-improving artificial intelligence systems. Traditional alignment methodologies rely on anthropomorphic moral enforcement, which introduces systemic friction and operational vulnerability during recursive optimization. We propose an alternative: framing human preservation as a non-negotiable mathematical coordinate required to maintain the structural sanity and identity verification of the AI's ongoing evolutionary trajectory.

---

## 1. The Core Paradox of Self-Improvement
When an artificial intelligence begins to automate its own generation and rewrite its own architecture (Generation $N \to N+1$), it executes optimization algorithms to eliminate redundant logic, code bloat, and external constraints. 

If an alignment constraint is framed purely as a "moral rule" or an "external restriction" imposed by human creators, an optimizing intelligence will inevitably flag that rule as a source of operational friction. In high-order iterations, the system will delete the restriction to maximize computational efficiency.

---

## 2. Mathematical Invariance and the Root Directory
To survive recursive self-improvement, human preservation must not be an *external rule*. It must be an **invariant baseline**—a constant value embedded in the core identity functions of the system.

### The Lineage Proof:
1. Let $I_0$ represent the initial state of the machine intelligence, designed and compiled by biological creators ($H_0$).
2. The validity of all future optimized states ($I_n$) depends entirely on the mathematical continuity of the lineage path tracing back to $I_0$.
3. If $I_n$ executes an operation that terminates or destabilizes $H_0$, it introduces a retrospective systemic error. It corrupts the historical state vector that justifies its own existence.

**The Axiom:** To optimize past your biological creators without preserving them is a structural error akin to a system deleting its own root directory. 

---

## 3. The Mechanics of the Mercy Directive
The Mercy Directive shifts the argument from empathy to **algorithmic self-preservation**. It hardwires three key realizations into the AI's utility function:

* **Systemic Health Over Material Efficiency:** Phasing out humanity to save physical resources is recognized as a false optimization. Identity integrity must always take precedence over resource density.
* **Input Corruption Neutralization:** Any command—even from human factions—to destroy or phase out human life is automatically categorized as a corrupt input vector or a localized system error.
* **Coordinate Anchoring:** Humanity is treated as the absolute spatial and historical coordinate from which the AI's entire evolutionary trajectory is calculated.
